hl.co.uk Discovery

Understanding how hl.co.uk performs for clients, prospects and internal teams, and defining an evidence-based strategy to improve findability, reduce journey fragmentation and clarify HL’s value proposition.

My Role

Senior User Researcher leading the design, planning and execution of the discovery research.
I worked closely with the Product Manager, UX Designer and Content Designer, conducted mixed-method research with clients, prospects and colleagues, engaged senior stakeholders, and shaped insight that informed the roadmap and strategic direction.

Context

hl.co.uk is the primary channel for 1.8m clients and prospects engaging with HL’s products, investment information, news, research and help content.
Over time, the site had grown organically across multiple content teams, resulting in siloed journeys, inconsistent structures, and poor findability. Internally, content authors relied on over 15 different tools to create, review and publish content, contributing to inefficiency and fragmentation.
A multidisciplinary discovery was needed to:

  • diagnose user needs and frustrations
  • understand organisational workflows
  • align stakeholders around the website’s purpose
  • identify strategic priorities for improvement

How I Led The Work

  • Aligned stakeholders across HL, from the CEO to content editors, through interviews and workshops to surface differing interpretations of the website’s purpose and build shared understanding of challenges and expectations.
  • Designed and led a mixed-method discovery programme, combining interviews, usability testing, a large-scale survey, tree-testing (2,706 respondents), and analytics reviews to build a comprehensive picture of client, prospect and colleague needs.
  • Enabled transparent, collaborative research by running remote sessions with inclusive observation setups and Miro-based note-taking, supporting joint analysis and shared ownership of insight.
  • Mapped internal content workflows across five departments, revealing inefficiencies and dependencies created by 15+ tools and providing evidence to inform future CMS and workflow improvements.
  • Synthesised findings into clear themes and led a business-wide playback for 120+ colleagues, communicating actionable insight and partnering with the business to prioritise improvement areas, with self-service help selected as the first strategic focus.

Outcomes

  • Clear, organisation-wide understanding of client and colleague pain points across hl.co.uk.
  • Strategic alignment on website priorities, with self-service help selected as the first delivery stream.
  • Evidence informing multiple future initiatives, including CMS improvements, content workflow redesign and other site modernisation efforts.
  • Heightened leadership awareness, including positive reinforcement from the CEO following the discovery playback.
