Customer Portal Vision & Discovery

Defining a self-serve vision that reduces dependancy on the Contact Centre, strengthens regulatory resilience, improves customer experience, and builds executive alignment around the need for digital investment.

My Role

Lead UX, Project Director.
I owned the end-to-end Discovery and vision definition, collaborating with senior leaders across Operations and Customer Service, and with a technology consultancy running an in-parallel technical Discovery.

Context

Post-merger, Bromford Flagship had scaled to 82k homes and 170k customers but remained reliant on high-cost, reactive service channels. The lack of self-serve capability was creating inconsistent customer experiences, increasing regulatory risk under the new Consumer Standards, and limiting the wider service redesign work underway across the integration programme.

How I Led The Work

  • Prioritised the highest value and highest-risk journeys through E2E task analysis across directorates, enabling Discovery efforts to focus on the greatest operational and customer impact.
  • Led customer research to create the evidence base for product decisions and stakeholder co-design. The work defined self-serve priorities, key journeys, IA structures, and surfaced behavioural-design opportunities (e.g. supporting customers to avoid arrears), reducing financial risk and shaping a more proactive future service model.
  • Developed and presented a platform vision narrative demonstrating customer and organisational impacts to the Board, securing buy-in and investment for delivery.

Outcomes

  • Secured £3m funding for a 2 year delivery programme following presentation to Board.
  • Shaped requirements for the subsequent procurement process, enabling Bromford Flagship to select an appropriate technology partner for delivery.
  • Built cross-directorate alignment and facilitated broader service and technology improvement discussions through an evidenced proof-of-concept.
