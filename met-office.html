Joe Marriott   Lead UX

2019  | Met Office

Redesigning the weather

Strengthening the Met Office’s ability to compete with leading commercial weather services by designing a modern, responsive service that meets the public’s weather information needs and improves engagement, retention and advertising revenue.

My Role

UX Lead and Project Director responsible for shaping the Discovery, Alpha and Beta approach.

I supported a UX Consultant and UX Designer, led the research and design direction, facilitated cross-disciplinary collaboration, and coordinated with Met Office product owners, editorial teams and meteorologists throughout the delivery phases.

Context

The Met Office’s digital estate was outdated, inefficient to maintain, and underperforming against competitors like the BBC, AccuWeather and The Weather Channel. As a public body reliant on advertising revenue to sustain its digital products, improving metoffice.gov.uk was strategically vital.

The site served an unusually broad audience, from daily commuters and dog walkers to ice-cream sellers, asthma sufferers and weather hobbyists, each with different motivations and use cases. Any redesign needed to reflect this diversity while remaining accessible, evidence-led and compliant with GDS standards.

How I Led The Work

  • Built a comprehensive understanding of public weather behaviours interviews, surveys and stakeholder insight, capturing diverse use cases and pain points across the UK.
  • Developed behavioural personas and a visual story map linking 40 user needs to scenarios and opportunities, ensuring the team designed for mindsets and tasks rather than fictitious “average” users.
  • Passed Discovery GDS assessment first time by presenting clear, evidence-based insight and a robust Alpha plan, reinforced by praise from assessors for exemplary user-centred practice.
  • Redesigned the website structure by benchmarking the existing IA, collaborating with editorial teams to define a new content model, and running tree-tests that increased findability for 31 of 40 key tasks.
  • Led iterative prototyping of key user journeys, facilitating sketching workshops, refining low-fidelity prototypes, and mapping components/CMS data requirements for development handover.
  • Created a user-centred innovation (“humanising the weather”) by working with Met Office scientists to design an algorithm that summarised forecast changes in plain language, improving comprehension and accessibility for screen-reader users.

Outcomes

Since the Beta launch, metoffice.gov.uk achieved significant improvements across key performance metrics:

  • 41% increase in new users
  • 38% increase in overall users (from 3.76m to 5.23m)
  • 33.6% increase in website sessions
  • 26% increase in page views
  • 6.5% increase in organic search acquisition

These results reflect increased engagement, improved usability, and a stronger platform for advertising-driven revenue.
