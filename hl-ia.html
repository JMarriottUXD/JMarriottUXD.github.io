IA & Navigation Redesign

Redesigning hl.co.uk’s navigation to make the site easier to use, strengthen how HL’s broader proposition and investment solutions are presented to prospects, and balance this with the needs of 1.8m existing clients to drive commercial performance across priority metrics.

My Role

Lead UX Consultant overseeing the end-to-end IA and navigation redesign across a 6-month engagement.

I led stakeholder alignment, research strategy, IA definition and iterative testing, and partnered closely with Proposition, SEO, Marketing, and Product teams to shape a solution that balanced user needs, commercial goals, and HL’s complex product landscape.

Context

hl.co.uk (one of the UK’s largest investment platforms) had not seen a major navigation update in over 7 years. The site had become sprawling and difficult to navigate, causing:

  • clients struggling to find essential content
  • prospects unable to understand HL’s proposition
  • a bloated nav limiting HL’s ability to drive engagement and performance

Remedying this required a structural overhaul and cultural shift within a risk-averse organisation with low experimentation maturity.

How I Led The Work

  • Aligned organisational perspectives by conducting 1:1 interviews and ideation sessions with 36 stakeholders (directors, proposition leads, marketing, SEO), using divergent IA concepts to spark discussion and clarify expectations for how HL’s proposition should be framed.
  • Defined and tested multiple IA concepts informed by analytics and stakeholder insight, conducting large-scale tree tests with 1,000+ clients to identify a high-performing top-level structure and diagnose depth-level issues.
  • Iterated the IA through further validation (two additional tree-testing rounds), achieving a level of findability performance that gave the business confidence to progress to implementation. (5 concepts tree-tested in total with 2,820 clients, plus 27 guerrilla tests with the new navigation UI.)
  • Co-designed a new primary navigation pattern grounded in principles such as progressive disclosure, clearer task pathways, and simplified search (moving from multiple fragmented searches to a combined model).
  • Led a risk-managed delivery approach, using a gradual 8-week rollout (5% to 25% to 50% to 100% of visitors), monitoring analytics and feedback, and making A/B-tested iterations during rollout to optimise performance and stakeholder confidence.

ADD CAPTION

Outcomes

All results compare old vs. new navigation over a controlled 2-month rollout window:

  • 13.13% increase in new account opens
  • 20.74% increase in transfers to HL, worth £53m AUA per month (calculated using an average transfer value of £41,840)
  • Successful deployment of the first major navigation change to hl.co.uk in more than seven years, with no drop in trading activity (a key risk metric).
